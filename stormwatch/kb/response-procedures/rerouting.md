# Rerouting Protocols

## Decision Framework

### Lead Time > 72 Hours
- RECOMMENDED: Full route deviation to alternate port
- South China Sea → Reroute to Singapore (adds 4–5 days)
- Gulf of Mexico → Reroute to Los Angeles (adds 7–10 days)
- North Atlantic → Southern crossing via Azores (adds 2 days)
- Success rate: 70–80% of cargo avoids significant delay

### Lead Time 24–72 Hours
- RECOMMENDED: Hold at origin or nearest safe port
- Vessels already in transit: adjust course to maximize distance from storm
- Alert downstream logistics partners of expected delay
- Success rate: 40–60% avoid major impact

### Lead Time < 24 Hours
- RECOMMENDED: Emergency protocols
- Seek nearest protected anchorage
- Expedite cargo insurance claims for Category 3+ within 200km
- Vessels with cargo >$5M: trigger pre-negotiated insurance terms
- Success rate: 10–20% avoid impact; focus shifts to damage minimization

### Lead Time < 12 Hours
- RECOMMENDED: Damage control mode
- Crew safety first: follow maritime emergency protocols
- Begin insurance documentation immediately
- Notify all downstream supply chain partners of expected disruption
- Historical data shows 90%+ delay rate at this lead time
