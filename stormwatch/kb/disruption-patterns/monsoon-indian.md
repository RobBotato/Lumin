# Monsoon & Cyclone Patterns — Indian Ocean

## Overview
The Indian Ocean experiences two monsoon seasons and an average of 5 cyclones
annually. The Mumbai–Durban and Singapore–Middle East corridors are most affected.

## Verified Impact Data

### Monsoon Flooding (June–September)
- Mumbai port: ~15% of operational days affected during monsoon
- Average delay per incident: 2–3 days
- Estimated annual cost to Indian shipping: $900M–1.2B

### Indian Ocean Cyclones (Category 2+)
- Typical shipping delay: 4–6 days
- Average financial impact: $900M–1.5B per event
- Historical precedent: Cyclone Idai (2019) — 6-day delay, $1.5B impact
- Durban port vulnerable: Mozambique Channel cyclones disrupt 30% of traffic

## Region-Specific Patterns
- Arabian Sea (5°–25°N, 55°–75°E): Cyclone risk May–June, October–November
- Bay of Bengal (5°–20°N, 80°–95°E): Most active cyclone basin in Indian Ocean
- Mozambique Channel (-15°–-25°S, 35°–45°E): Peak January–March

## Mitigation Effectiveness
- Pre-stocking cargo at Singapore reduces downstream Indian Ocean impact by 35%
- Mumbai alternate routing via Jeddah adds 4 days but avoids monsoon closures
