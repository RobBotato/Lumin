# Hurricane Patterns — Atlantic & Gulf of Mexico

## Overview
The Atlantic hurricane season (June–November) generates 14 named storms annually
on average. The Gulf of Mexico is the most vulnerable region for US-bound
shipping, with Houston port being the primary risk point.

## Verified Impact Data

### Category 2 Hurricanes
- Typical shipping delay: 4–6 days for Gulf routes
- Port closure duration: 2–4 days (Houston, New Orleans)
- Average financial impact: $1.0B–1.8B per event

### Category 3+ Hurricanes
- Typical shipping delay: 7–10 days for Gulf routes
- Port closure duration: 4–8 days
- Average financial impact: $3.2B–8.0B per event
- Historical precedent: Hurricane Harvey (2017) — 10-day delay, $8B impact
- Historical precedent: Hurricane Dorian (2019) — 7-day delay, $3.4B impact
- Houston port closures caused 14-day backlog after Harvey

## Region-Specific Patterns
- Gulf of Mexico (25°–30°N, 85°–95°W): Peak August–October
- North Atlantic (40°–50°N, 30°–50°W): Peak December–February (winter storms)
- Caribbean bypass routes add 5–8 days to South America–US routes

## Mitigation Effectiveness
- Rerouting to Los Angeles/Long Beach: Adds 7–10 days for Atlantic cargo
- Early rerouting (72h+ lead time): Can save an estimated $800M per major storm
- Pre-positioning cargo at alternate ports reduces downstream backlog by 40%
